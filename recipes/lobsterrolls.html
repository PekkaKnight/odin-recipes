Lobster Rolls

Description

Lobster Rolls were created at Perry's, a restaurant in Mildford, Connecticut all the way back in 1927, inspired by a similar dish from New England. It's a wonderful summer and winter treat and makes a fancy lunch that will surely impress your friends. You can make this lobster for less than half the price of the ones you could buy in a store or eat in a restaurant, and it's just as delicious, if not better.

Ingredients

  • Buttered Rolls: Lightly butter hot dog buns or kaiser rolls.
  • Vegetables: Lettuce leaves, green onions, and celery.
  • Mayonaise: If you want some creamy, savory sauce.
  • Lime juice: Lime juice lends brightness to the mayo-based sauce mentioned in this list. You can substitute lemon juice if you prefer.
  • Seasonings: The sauce is flavored with hot sauce, salt, and pepper. The lobster is topped with dried basil, tarragon, or parsley.
  • Lobster: You will need one and a half pounds(1.5) of cooked and cured lobster meat to make four lobster rolls.
