Asian Lettuce Wrap

Description

These lettuce wraps were believed to have originated in China. They were made popular in P.F. Changs and now there are multiple resources saying how to make them. They are easy to recreate at home using any style you want.

Ingredients

  • 16 Boston Bibb or Butter Lettuce Leaves
  • 1 pound lean ground beef
  • 1 Tablespoon cooking oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • A quarter clup hoisin sauce
  • 2 cloves fresh garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice wine vinegar
  • 2 teaspoons minced pickled ginger
  • 1 dash Asian chile pepper sauce, or to taste(Your choice)
  • 1(8 ounce) can water chestnuts, drained, and finely chopped
  • 1 bunch green onions, chopped
  • 2 teaspoons Asian(dark) sesame oil

Steps

  1. Rinse whole lettuce leaves and pat dry. Be careful not to tear them. Set aside when done
  2. Heat oil in a large skillet over-medium high heat. Add beef, cook and steer until browned and crumbly, 5-7 minutes. Drain and discard grease; transfer beef to a bowl. Add onion to the same skillet, cook and stir until just tender, 5-7 minutes.
  3. Stir in hoisin sauce, garlic, soy sauce, vinegar, ginger, and chili pepper sauce until well combined. Add water chestnut, green onions, sesame oil, and cooked beef; cook and steer until onions just begin to wilt, about 2 minutes
  4. Arrange lettuce leaves around the outer edge of a large serving platter; spoon beef mixture in the center.
